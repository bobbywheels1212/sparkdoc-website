Try This Before You Call

About half of dead outlet calls we get can be resolved in 30 seconds with one of these steps:

💡 Check Your GFCI Outlets First

GFCI outlets (the ones with TEST and RESET buttons in your bathroom, kitchen, garage, or outside) can cut power to other outlets on the same circuit when they trip. Find any GFCI outlet in your home and press the RESET button firmly. This often brings dead outlets back instantly.

  • Check the breaker panel — look for any breaker in the middle (tripped) position and reset it
  • Find every GFCI outlet in your home and press RESET (bathroom, kitchen, garage, exterior)
  • Try a different device — make sure it's not the device that's the problem
  • Check for scorch marks or burning smell — if present, don't use the outlet and call immediately

If none of that works, the problem is inside the outlet or wiring — and that's where we come in.

Common Causes We Find

When a simple GFCI reset doesn't fix it, here's what we typically find:

  • Loose wire connection — The wires connecting to the back of the outlet terminals have worked loose over time. Common in homes over 15 years old.
  • Burned or failed outlet — The outlet itself has worn out or been damaged by a power surge. Outlets have a finite lifespan.
  • Upstream wiring fault — A break in the wiring between the breaker and the outlet — inside the wall or at a junction box.
  • Aluminum wiring — Homes built in the late 1960s–70s may have aluminum wiring, which requires special outlets and connections. Standard outlets can fail or become fire hazards when used with aluminum wiring.

Common Questions

Dead Outlet FAQ

Why did my outlet stop working?
The most common reason is a tripped GFCI outlet somewhere on the same circuit — often in a bathroom, kitchen, or garage. Other causes include a tripped circuit breaker, a loose wire connection behind the outlet, a burned-out outlet, or a wiring fault further back in the circuit.
What is a GFCI outlet and why does it trip?
A GFCI outlet detects tiny current leaks to ground and shuts off power in milliseconds to prevent electrocution. They're required in bathrooms, kitchens, garages, and outdoor areas. One tripped GFCI can cut power to multiple outlets on the same circuit. Look for the outlet with TEST and RESET buttons and press RESET.
How do I fix a dead outlet?
Check your breaker panel for a tripped breaker. Then check every GFCI outlet in your home and press the RESET button. If that doesn't restore power, the outlet or its wiring needs professional diagnosis.
Can a dead outlet be a fire hazard?
Yes. A dead outlet caused by a loose wire connection can also be arcing inside the wall. If you notice scorch marks, smell burning, or the outlet feels warm, call an electrician immediately — don't use that outlet.
How much does it cost to fix a dead outlet?
