Signs You Need a Dedicated Circuit Now

If a breaker trips every time you run a specific appliance, lights dim when you turn it on, or you smell burning near an outlet — that circuit is overloaded. Running high-draw appliances on a shared circuit isn't just inconvenient, it's a fire hazard over time.

  • Correct wire gauge for the load — no shortcuts
  • Breaker sized and labeled correctly
  • Outlet matched to the application (NEMA 14-50 for EVs, etc.)

Common Questions

Dedicated Circuit FAQ

What appliances need a dedicated circuit?
Electric ranges, ovens, refrigerators, dishwashers, washers, dryers, microwaves, garbage disposals, EV chargers, hot tubs, HVAC equipment, and high-draw workshop tools all typically require dedicated circuits per the NEC.
How do I know if I need a dedicated circuit?
A breaker that trips when you run a specific appliance, lights that dim when something turns on, or an appliance manual that specifies a dedicated circuit are all clear signs. Call us and we'll diagnose it.
How long does it take to add a dedicated circuit?
Most installs take 1–3 hours depending on distance from the panel, wall/attic accessibility, and whether the panel has open slots. If the panel is full, we can discuss options.
Do I need a permit to add a dedicated circuit?
For EV chargers, hot tubs, and subpanels, yes — permits are typically required in Kansas and Missouri. We handle permitting when required and let you know upfront.
What if my panel is full?
Options include tandem breakers to free up space, adding a subpanel for a specific area, or a full panel upgrade. We'll assess and recommend the most practical solution for your situation.
