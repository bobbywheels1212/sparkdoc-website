What Counts as an Electrical Emergency?

These situations require an immediate call — do not wait until morning:

Sparking or Arcing

Visible sparks from outlets, switches, or your panel are an immediate fire hazard.

Burning Smell

An electrical burning smell with no visible source often means wiring is overheating inside a wall.

Complete Power Loss

Total power loss when your neighbors still have power — especially if it can't be explained by a tripped main breaker.

Exposed Wiring

Wiring that has been damaged, cut, or is exposed — from storms, pests, or construction accidents.

Flooding Near Panel

Water in or near your electrical panel or service entrance is an immediate electrocution hazard.

Buzzing in Walls

Crackling or buzzing sounds from walls or the panel are the sound of arcing — don't ignore it.

What to Do Right Now

  • 1 If you see smoke or flames — leave immediately and call 911. Do not try to address an electrical fire with water.
  • 2 Keep everyone away from the affected area, panel, or outlets.
  • 3 Do not reset sparking or smoking breakers. Do not touch exposed wires.
  • 4 Call Spark Doc Electric at (913) 390-3780 — we're available 24/7.

Is It an Emergency or Can It Wait?

Not sure? Call us anyway — we'll help you figure it out. As a general guide:

Can't wait: sparking, burning smell, smoke, total power loss, exposed wires, flooding near panel.

Usually can wait until morning: single dead outlet, flickering in one room, tripped breaker that resets normally, lights out in one area with no smell or sparking.

Common Questions

Emergency Electrician FAQ

What counts as an electrical emergency?
Sparks or flames from outlets or the panel, a burning smell with no visible source, complete power loss, exposed or damaged wiring, flooding near your panel, buzzing or crackling from walls, or a breaker that won't reset and controls a critical system like HVAC or medical equipment.
What should I do during an electrical emergency?
If you see smoke or flames: leave immediately and call 911. For other emergencies: don't touch exposed wires, don't reset a sparking or smoking panel, keep people away from the affected area, and call (913) 390-3780.
How fast can you get to me in Olathe or Kansas City?
Response time depends on your location and time of day. We serve all of Johnson County KS, Jackson County MO, and Wyandotte County KS. Call (913) 390-3780 and we'll give you an honest ETA.
Is my electrical issue an emergency or can it wait?
If in doubt, call us — it's always better to ask. True emergencies (sparking, burning smell, total power loss, exposed wires) should not wait. Issues like a single dead outlet, flickering in one room, or a tripped breaker that resets normally can typically wait for a next-day appointment.
