What Causes a Panel to Spark?

Sparking happens when electricity jumps (arcs) across a gap between two conductive surfaces that should be in solid contact. In a breaker panel, the most common causes are:

  • Loose wire lugs — Wires connecting to breakers or bus bars that have loosened over time, allowing arcing at the connection point.
  • Worn or failing breakers — Breakers that no longer snap firmly into the bus bar lose proper contact and can arc.
  • Moisture or corrosion — Water intrusion or condensation creates conductive paths between components that shouldn't touch.
  • Recalled panel brands — Federal Pacific (Stab-Lok) and Zinsco panels are known to fail to trip under overload and are prone to arcing. If you have one of these, consider replacement regardless of symptoms.

Panel Repair vs. Replacement

Not every sparking panel needs full replacement. A single failing breaker can be swapped out quickly. But if the panel itself shows signs of heat damage, the bus bars are corroded, or you have a recalled brand, replacement is the safer long-term choice. Common Questions

Sparking Panel FAQ

Is a sparking breaker panel dangerous?
Yes — extremely. A sparking panel means electricity is arcing across connections that shouldn't be arcing. This generates extreme heat that can melt wiring insulation, ignite surrounding materials, and start a fire inside your walls. Call a licensed electrician immediately.
What causes a breaker panel to spark?
Common causes: loose wire connections or bus bar lugs that allow electricity to arc; a failing or worn breaker that no longer makes solid contact; corrosion or moisture inside the panel; or an overloaded circuit causing heat buildup and eventual arcing.
What should I do if my breaker panel is sparking?
Do not touch the panel. Do not try to reset breakers. If you smell burning or see smoke, leave the house and call 911. If there's no immediate smoke or fire, call a licensed electrician right away — (913) 390-3780.
What does buzzing or crackling from a breaker panel mean?
Buzzing or crackling is the sound of electrical arcing — electricity jumping across a loose or failing connection. Both sounds and visible sparks should be treated as emergencies.
Can a sparking panel be repaired or does it need to be replaced?
It depends on the cause. A single failing breaker can often be replaced. But if the panel itself is damaged, the bus bars are corroded, or it's a recalled brand (Federal Pacific or Zinsco), full replacement is usually the right call. We'll give you an honest recommendation after diagnosis.
