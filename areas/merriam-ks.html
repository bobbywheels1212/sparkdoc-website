Electrician in Merriam — Common Questions

My Merriam home still has a fuse box — is that dangerous?
A fuse box isn't automatically dangerous, but it does present real limitations. Homes built in the 1950s and 1960s were typically wired for 60-amp service — far less than today's standard 200-amp. If your home has been updated with modern appliances, central air conditioning, or any added circuits over the years, the original service may be inadequate. We'll inspect what you have and give you a clear picture of the risk level and your options.
How much does a panel upgrade cost in Merriam?
Panel upgrade costs vary depending on your current service size, the work needed to bring the new panel up to current code, and whether any associated wiring needs to be addressed. We provide transparent, itemized quotes before any work begins — no surprises. Call (913) 390-3780 and we'll discuss your specific situation.
Do older Merriam homes need GFCI outlets?
Yes. GFCI (ground fault circuit interrupter) protection is required by current electrical code in bathrooms, kitchens, garages, and outdoor areas — and homes built before the 1970s almost never have it. Adding GFCI protection to an older Merriam home is one of the most cost-effective safety upgrades we do, and it's often a requirement when selling the home.
Can you rewire just part of my house, or does it have to be all at once?
Partial rewires are very common in Merriam's older homes. We can prioritize the circuits or areas that present the most risk or cause the most problems, and address the rest over time. We'll give you a full picture of what we find and help you make a smart plan that fits your budget and timeline.
