My Kansas City, MO home is from the 1930s — what electrical issues should I expect?

Homes from that era often have low-capacity panels (60 amps or less), outdated cloth-covered wiring, limited outlet coverage, and little to no GFCI protection. Some may still have remnants of knob-and-tube wiring in attic or basement spaces. We'll assess everything honestly and help you prioritize the work — the goal is always making the home safe first, then adding capacity for modern living.

Can you upgrade the panel in my Kansas City, MO home to add an EV charger?

Absolutely. Panel upgrades paired with EV charger installations are one of the most common projects we do throughout the metro, including on the Missouri side. We'll assess your current service entry, upgrade the panel to 200 amps if needed, and install the Level 2 charger circuit in a single coordinated job. We pull all required permits through Kansas City's permitting office.
