Electrician in Shawnee — Common Questions

My Shawnee home still has the original 100-amp panel — is that a problem?

It can be. Many Shawnee homes from the 1970s and 1980s were built with 100-amp service, which was fine at the time but often falls short today. If you're experiencing tripped breakers, flickering lights, or you want to add an EV charger or major appliance, a 200-amp panel upgrade is likely the right move. We'll inspect your current setup and give you an honest assessment.

Do you handle panel upgrades for older Shawnee homes?

Yes — panel upgrades are one of our most common jobs in Shawnee. We upgrade 100-amp panels to 200 amps (or 400 amps for larger homes), pull all required permits through Johnson County, and coordinate any utility work needed with Evergy. The whole process usually takes one day.

How fast can you get to my home in Shawnee?

We're based in Olathe, so Shawnee is typically about 10 minutes away. For same-day calls, we can usually arrive within a couple of hours. For electrical emergencies — sparking panels, burning smells, complete power loss — we respond 24/7 and prioritize getting to you as fast as possible.

Can you install an EV charger in my Shawnee garage?

Absolutely. We install Level 2 (240V) EV chargers in Shawnee garages and driveways. If your current panel has capacity, we can often add the circuit the same day. If a panel upgrade is needed first, we can scope the whole project together and give you one price for everything.
