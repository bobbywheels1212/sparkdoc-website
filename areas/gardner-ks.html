Electrician in Gardner — Common Questions

My new construction home in Gardner already has 200-amp service — do I still need an upgrade to add an EV charger?
Not necessarily an upgrade, but you do need a dedicated 240-volt circuit run to your garage. Many Gardner builders leave the panel with room to spare, so adding a 50-amp circuit for a Level 2 EV charger is typically a straightforward job. We'll check your available capacity and let you know exactly what's needed before any work begins.
I bought an older home near downtown Gardner — what electrical issues should I expect?
Homes built before the 1980s in Gardner's older neighborhoods often have 60- or 100-amp panels that weren't designed for today's electrical loads. You may also find outdated receptacles, missing GFCI protection in bathrooms and kitchens, and wiring that has aged past its useful life. We offer a thorough safety inspection so you know exactly what you have before problems develop.
Do you pull permits for electrical work in Gardner?
Yes. Gardner falls under the jurisdiction of the City of Gardner for permits, and we handle the permit application and inspection scheduling as part of the job. Panel upgrades, new circuits, EV charger installations, and significant wiring work all typically require permits — we take care of it.
